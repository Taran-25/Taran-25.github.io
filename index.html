About

How I think about product

Philosophy

Good product work starts with an uncomfortable question: why does this problem exist and who actually owns it? I try to stay close to that question before touching a solution. Most bad products are built by people who skipped it.

What I've learned shipping

Running Food Holic taught me that retention beats acquisition every time, and that feedback loops only work if you're honest about what the data is actually saying. At Discovr, I learned what it takes to drive a product 0→1 — and that shipping fast only holds up when attribution and compliance are designed in from day one, not bolted on later.
