About

How I think about product

Philosophy

Good product work starts with an uncomfortable question: why does this problem exist and who actually owns it? I try to stay close to that question before touching a solution. Most bad products are built by people who skipped it.

What I've learned shipping

Running Food Holic taught me that retention beats acquisition every time, and that customer feedback loops only work if you're honest about what the data is actually saying. At ZopDev, I learned that a sharp pain point framed correctly travels further than a feature list ever could.
